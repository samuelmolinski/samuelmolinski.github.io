    • Met Oct. 11th, with UL and David Thibodeaux STEM academy to plan fabrication
      • David Thibodeaux students will be available to help with fabrication in November through January
        • Tentative schedule
          • CGI will hold a tutorial for high school students on Nov. 13th

  • LCG, LUS Fiber and Network/Hardware Planning update 
    • Private LUS sensor network will be terminated in Stephens Hall at UL
    • Gene Fields CIO is committed to doing what we need in Stephens Hall
    • Plan to use GENIE hardware provided by UL for Gateway
      • Delcambre, Matthew (University of Louisiana at Lafayette) cleared this usage with US Ignite
      • Delcambre, Matthew (University of Louisiana at Lafayette) will check if we can place our own gateway in Stephens
      • GENIE rack is ready for production as of 6/22
      • Easiest way to use these is to run Docker containers
    • 3 potential Network Topologies
      • Option 1: Use existing LCG traffic network
        • Diagram: https://alm.cgifederal.com/collaborate/download/attachments/66586591/LEaRN-option1.pdf?version=1&modificationDate=1501604955000&api=v2
        • Pros: Cheapest
        • Cons: Security concerns
      • Option 2: New LEaRN VPLS via LUSFiber
        • Diagram: https://alm.cgifederal.com/collaborate/download/attachments/66586591/LEaRN-option2.pdf?version=1&modificationDate=1501604965000&api=v2
        • Stevens Hall would be termination point for this network
        • Pros: Secure
        • Cons: May requires purchase of switches/router to limit broadcast traffic (may be able to work around this), requires network costs
      • Option 3: LEaRN commercial Internet, one connection per traffic cabinet
        • Diagram: https://alm.cgifederal.com/collaborate/download/attachments/66586591/LEaRN-option3.pdf?version=1&modificationDate=1501604973000&api=v2
        • Pros: More secure than option 1, Would not require purchase of switches
        • Cons: Less secure of option 2, requires network costs
    • As of Oct. 11th, LUS reports they have identified a path forward.
      • Terry Huval arranging meeting w/Mayor-President to work through the details
        • Hopefully this will happen in the next few weeks
      • We will schedule a follow-up LEaRN-LUS networking meeting after the meeting with the Mayor-President

  • LCG Data Access
    • Desired data:

      •  Distribution of traffic congestion and its health effects on:

        • Households within 1/4 mile of heavy traffic roadways
        • Heavy pedestrian areas (walking, biking, etc.)
        • Those with pre-existing conditions like asthma, heart diseases, and  lung diseases
        • Distance from parks, schools, and nursing homes
      • Traffic Data requests:
        • Annual Average Daily Traffic 
        • Traffic Count (Monthly Volume)
        • Traffic Count (Hourly Volume)
        • Directional Hourly Volume by Vehicle Class
        • Pedestrian traffic (location of crosswalks)
        • Cyclist traffic (location of bicycle lanes)

      • Health Data requests:

        • Citizens with asthma, heart and/or lung diseases (preferably broken down by address)
        • Annual averages of citizens with temporary respiratory issues (Upper respiratory infection, bronchitis, outdoor allergies etc.)
        • Citizens with autoimmune diseases and the correlation to high humidity/low barometric pressured areas
